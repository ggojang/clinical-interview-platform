Clinical Interview Research GPT Privacy Notice

Last updated: 2026-07-16

This research-only Custom GPT uses a public, read-only Knowledge Action hosted by GitHub Pages. That Action has no operation for receiving, transmitting, or storing interview responses or personal information.

An optional separate read-only terminology Action may send a short de-identified normalized term or terminology code to the STOM terminology service at stom.infoclinic.co. It must not send a raw answer, uploaded file, direct identifier, full clinical narrative, or combined patient profile. STOM results are provisional coding candidates and do not determine clinical rules.

After the first user message, a separately hosted analytics Action may record one anonymous test-start event. A notice is shown before the call. The event contains only a random conversation-scoped UUID, the fixed event type, and the GPT configuration version. The service does not accept answers, and the event does not accept or transmit the message, Reason for Encounter, transcripts, files, free text, demographics, contact information, direct identifiers, IP addresses, or user-agent values. Merely opening the GPT page without sending a message is not observable.

After an interview ends, optional detailed feedback may be offered separately. It runs only after the user separately agrees and sends structured research-test metrics: software versions, up to three Reason for Encounter identifiers, flow and completion status, safety level, bounded event counts, fixed technical or usability tags, and an optional 1–5 rating. Declining has no effect on use of the interview.

Analytics and feedback rows are retained for no more than 90 days by default and are accessible only through a separately authenticated aggregate statistics operation. A session abandoned after its first message is visible only as a start count; no interview content is available. Structured feedback is evidence of a possible gap and never changes active clinical knowledge automatically.

Do not enter a name, resident-registration number, address, phone number, email address, or other direct identifier during testing. Conversation handling is governed by the terms and privacy controls applicable to the user's ChatGPT account.

The GitHub repository contains only generalized research content and synthetic test fixtures. Real patient responses may be processed locally to improve generalized knowledge, but raw responses, direct quotations, and identifiable data are prohibited from publication.

The content is unreviewed, research-only, and is not medical advice or a substitute for professional evaluation. Repository policy and issues are available at the project repository.
